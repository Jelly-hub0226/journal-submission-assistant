# Article Quality Scoring Rubric

## Dimension 1: Topic Novelty (25%, score 1-10)

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1-3 | Well-trodden topic; no new angle or data; replicates known findings |
| 4-6 | Topic has existing literature but offers a new context, population, or minor twist |
| 7-8 | Addresses a genuine research gap; timely and relevant to current debates |
| 9-10 | Groundbreaking; opens a new sub-field or paradigm shift; highly original research question |

Look for: Does the introduction clearly state what gap this fills? Is the topic currently trending in the field? Would reviewers find the question interesting?

## Dimension 2: Method Rigor (30%, score 1-10)

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1-3 | Method inappropriate or unjustified; obvious flaws (tiny sample, no controls, cherry-picked data) |
| 4-6 | Acceptable method but limited justification; sample size marginal; potential confounds not addressed |
| 7-8 | Well-chosen method with solid justification; adequate sample; limitations acknowledged |
| 9-10 | Methodologically exemplary; large/representative sample; robust controls; replicable; pre-registered if applicable |

For theoretical/philosophical papers (no empirical data): evaluate logical rigor, comprehensiveness of argument, and engagement with counter-arguments instead of empirical methods.

## Dimension 3: Argument Depth (25%, score 1-10)

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1-3 | Superficial; claims unsupported; no theoretical grounding |
| 4-6 | Basic argument present but underdeveloped; limited engagement with literature |
| 7-8 | Coherent, well-supported argument; engages meaningfully with existing scholarship |
| 9-10 | Deep theoretical insight; transforms understanding of the topic; anticipates and refutes counter-arguments |

## Dimension 4: Writing Quality (20%, score 1-10)

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 1-3 | Poor structure; major language errors; unclear at paragraph level |
| 4-6 | Readable but rough; some organizational or language issues |
| 7-8 | Clear, well-structured; minor language polishing needed |
| 9-10 | Publication-ready prose; elegant structure; engaging and precise |

## Weighted Total Formula

```
Total = (Novelty x 0.25) + (Method x 0.30) + (Argument x 0.25) + (Writing x 0.20)
```
Result is a score from 1.0 to 10.0.

## Interpretation Guide

| Total Score | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| 1.0-3.9 | Early-stage work; needs substantial revision before submission |
| 4.0-5.5 | Suitable for general/unranked journals or conferences |
| 5.6-7.0 | Competitive for mid-tier indexed journals |
| 7.1-8.5 | Strong candidate for reputable indexed journals (Q2-Q1) |
| 8.6-10.0 | Potentially suitable for top-tier/high-impact journals |

**Important**: These are heuristic guidelines. Journal fit depends on scope match as much as quality score.
